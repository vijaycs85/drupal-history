Drupal 20

2020

Code contribution via issue forks

Drupal contributors may now collaborate using git branches and merge requests. Until this day, code collaboration on drupal.org featured uploaded patch files.

Drupal 9.0 released 9.0

The first Drupal release with no new functionality - and that's great! Drupal 8 committed to adding functionality in a backwards compatible manner. Drupal 9 has the same functionality as Drupal 8.9, but without a lot of backward compatibility code. This approach balances easy major version major upgrades with a lean codebase. https://celebratedrupal.org/ centralized our virtual parties, as this happened during the days of Covid.

2017

Drupal changes backwards compatibility policy after 16 years.

Drupal was doing Move Fast and Break Things a few years before Facebook. New features were routinely committed to Drupal without backward compatibility. Recognizing its large installed base, Drupal announces that all changes must be backward compatible, and that major version upgrades will be easy forever.

2016

PHP 7 is released

A major language renewal comes with the new Zend Engine 3 (PHPNG), Itâ€™s also twice as fast as the previous PHP 5.6 release.

Cheney-Stauss Unofficial Drupalcon After Party

Drupal's fun/art side thrives at these late night Drupalcon parties. This year Drupalers chatted among fire throwers and wild art installations at the Intergalactic Krewe of Chewbacchus in New Orleans.

2015

Drupal 8.0 released 8.0

Five years in the making, Drupal 8 embraces objects and declarative configuration. Entity API, Twig, and Accessibility are the talk at the worldwide release parties.

Cache API boosts Drupal 8 performance

wimleers and fabianx rescue Drupal 8 performance with an API where Drupal caches rendered content, varying the keys as specified by a developer. Cache tags precisely invalidate related content as edits are submitted.

drupal-project demonstrates how to assemble a codebase via Composer

webflo showed the world how to build a Drupal codebase with Composer. Prior to this day some sites assembled code with Drush Make. drupal.org later built upon this work for its Packagist facade and Composer templates.

Drupalcon Prenote.

jam & robertDouglass wake us up for an infotainment extravaganza featuring sing-a-longs; bad jokes; Git tutorials, yodelling, use of alphabet, or beer pouring technique. On this day we belted out a Drupal flavored Let it Go from Frozen.

Drupal core governance overhaul for project sustainability.

Drupal had always relied on a single release manager to review and merge core changes. On this day Drupal embraces multiple committer roles and adds a process for adding new committers. In 2020 are about a dozen core committers.

2014

Australian government standardizes on Drupal.

govCMS embodies Drupal's popularity with governments all over the world.

SA-CORE-2014-005 - Drupal core - SQL injection

Many thousands of Drupal sites are compromised by Drupal core's first major security vulnerability.

2013

Drupal 8 gets semver, minor release scheduling, and beta requirements policy

Drupal core adopts semantic versioning and a sustainable, predictable minor release schedule.

2012

Symfony components merged into Drupal

Crell leads a push to replace Drupal's bootstrap with Symfony flavored request handling. This large change nearly assassinated Drupal 8, but the mighty Drop prevailed

2011

Drupalcon Trivia Night

Alan Burke proposes an in-person, team based trivia contest on the last night of Drupalcon Chicago. Drupalers have a jolly time, nobody recalls who wins, and Irish Drupal hosts many more trivia nights.

Drupal celebrates its 10 Anniversary in style

Drupalcon Chicago culminates in a dinner party at the Field Museum of Natural History. Dining with us in the main hall is Sue, the world's most complete Tyrannosaurus rex. Kudos to Tiffany and George from Palantir for a great Drupalcon and party.

Automated testing for Drupal Core and Contrib

After 7 years in Contrib, the simpletest module is added in Drupal 7, along with a substantial test suite. qa.drupal.org and then DrupalCI ensure that each patch passes tests before merging. Drupal Test Traits later brings this framework to client sites.

Drupal 7.0 released 7.0

More than 250 parties in over 90 countries were held to celebrate this great release. Drupal 7 is still the most popular Drupal release, featuring the Seven theme, designed by Mark Boulton and Leisa Reichelt.

Core Office Hours and DrupalCon Mentoring

xjm and catch begin leading core office hours on IRC 12 hours apart, providing global coverage. This generous spirit soon manifests at DrupalCon Denver as the first Core Mentoring contribution day, where new contributors are guided through their first core issue.

2010

Drupal development moves from CVS to Git

webchick opens a two week discussion assessing bzr and git for our next VCS. Then she reviews the discussion and announces that Git has been chosen. This is how bike sheds are overcome.

Certified to Rock web service launches

Certified To Rock is a new form of certification built to match the unique nature of the Drupal community. Give back more, and your score rises.

Open Atrium distro by Development Seed

An early distro, and a shining light in terms of visual design, user experience, and software architecture.

2009

Pressflow friendly fork adds DB replication and reverse proxy caching

David Strauss and collaborators maintain a distribution that helps Drupal scale. Their improvements are later incorporated into Drupal 7.

Drush becomes stand-alone tool with 2.0 release

Drush is no longer a module, helping it install a site, perform DB backups, etc. Drush becomes Drupal's de facto command line tool.

2008

Drupal 6.0 released 6.0

This release features a rewritten menu system, and an Update Status module which prompts admins to update their modules, especially after a security release.

2007

Sun Microsystems bails out drupal.org

Drupal.org becomes too popular for its home grown web hosting, and starts throwing errors regularly. Sun Microsystems donates its servers and restores stability to our home.

Acquia is announced to the world

Acquia, a startup by Dries Buytaert and Jay Batson, announces its intention to offer commercial support and hosting for Drupal. Acquia continues to be the largest sponsor of Drupal development, and its largest web hosting platform.

Bay Area Drupal Camp (BADCamp) unites Drupalers at UC Berkeley.

This large, annual event remains free to this day. Kudos to Jen Lampton for her many years of organizing. Other notable Camps include Design 4 Drupal, Drupal Dev Days, Frontend United, and Midwest Developer Summit

Drupal 5.0 is released 5.0

This release incorporates jQuery, a young open source project. Drupal's support helps jQuery gain attention and then it rises to huge popularity. This release introduces install profiles and a web based installer.

2006

Views module is released

Earl Miles introduces Views, a query builder and list builder UI. Along with CCK, these form the early beating heart of Drupal. Views graduates into Drupal core with D8.

Drupal Association formalizes governance and money management.

Drupal's governance formalizes from BDFL to a non-profit model. Led by Jacob Redding, VZW is initially a money manager only. Drupal VZW transforms into the Drupal Association in 2011 and becomes more prominent in promoting Drupal and runs Drupalcon.

Drupal 4.7 is released with a Form API. 4.7

The new Form API by Adrian Rossouw strengthens Drupal's secure-by-default approach. XSS protections, validation of choices, and only processing POST data that was actually on the form.

Content Creation Kit (CCK) by yched and KarenS, adds custom fields to nodes via the Drupal UI.

CCK was and is the heart of Drupal's rich content type system. CCK moved into Drupal core in v7, and is now known as the Field API. JonBob led a session about it at very first Drupalcon, and made the first iteration in Flexinode module, which later morphed into CCK.

groups.drupal.org is launched

Moshe Weitzman introduced this portal for local user groups to have an online rallying place. Groups helped the Drupal community grow tighter, and helped newbies climb its learning curve. Later led by joshk, greggles, and ezra-g.

Lullabot podcast is launched

This podcast, founded by Jeff Robbins, still runs today. This marks a growth in the marketing and communication of Drupal.

2005

'Building Online Communities' is published

Robert Douglass authors the first Drupal book, recognizing a growing demand for Drupal education.

Drupal security team is formed

This volunteer team is first led by chx. The team dutifully protects Drupal core and all the contrib modules that opt into its coverage. Future leaders would be Heine, greggles, and mlhess.

First Drupalcon held in Antwerp.

Drupal enthusiasts meet in person for the first time. Amazingly, 45 people fly to Antwerp from all over the globe. Marc Canter (Macromedia founder) and Chris Musssina (later invented the #hashtag) attended. The conference with scheduled presentations happened at FOSDEM on Feb 26th, 2005. Dries' Album

2003

Drupalers build Deanspace, a semi-official presence for USA presidential candidate Howard Dean.

This was a milestone in the rise of the Internet for political campaigns and grassroots initiatives. It also kicked off a long love affair between Drupal and non-profits.

2002

Drupal 4.0 is released, with a new taxonomy module. 4.0

Taxonomy module, written by Marco Molinari. Marco wrote it because he needed a way to group forums into categories. Drupal's rich taxonomy system foreshadows what will be popularly known as tags/labels.

2001

Drupal 3.0 is released. 3.0

3.0 major had a whopping 6 month development cycle. Nodes are introduced as a building block for content. They can be specialized into many content types.

Druplicon is introduced

Drupal's famous logo, featuring infinity eyes and remixed into thousands of variants, is introduced by Steven Wittens and Kristjan Jansen.

Drupal 2.0 is released 2.0

A mere 2 months after 1.0, Drupal ships 2.0. Backward compatibility was not yet a concern. The translation system is introduced, with its brilliantly simple t() function.

Drupal 1.0 is released 1.0

Dries Buytaert shares the initial release of his 'dormitory bulletin board'. It includes 18 files that are called modules. It features a hook system where modules interact with each other and the core framework. On this very day, Wikipedia is also born. Had that happened 6 months later, it would probably be a Drupal site!.
